Alphabet

  • b - /β/, /b/ if it comes after "m" in a syllable
  • d - /ð/
  • f - /f/, /p̪/ if it comes after "m" in a syllable
  • v - /v/, /b̪/ if it comes after "m" in a syllable
  • s - /s/
  • z - /z/
  • ç - /ç/, /c/ if it comes after "m" in a syllable
  • j - /ʝ/, /ɟ/ if it comes after "m" in a syllable
  • m - /m/, /ɱ/ if it comes before "f" or "v" in a syllable
  • r - /ɾ/
  • i - /i/
  • y - /y/
  • e - /e/
  • ê - /æ/, replaces an "e" that comes after "m" or "r"

Digraphs

  • hç - /cç/
  • hj - /ɟʝ/

Phonotactics

C1(r/m)V1(V2)(m)(C2) where

  • C1: any consonant
  • r/m: only if C1 isn't r, m, hç, or hj
  • V1: any vowel (except cannot be e after m or r, use ê instead)
  • V2: must be same vowel as V1 if second m exists
  • m: only if V1 and V2 both exist and are the same vowel
  • C2: any consonant except r, m, hç or hj
  • Stress always on second syllable of root

Genders: zmêfmy, brêd, zys, bmihji, fri

Numbers: singular, dual, plural

Noun endings

  • Singular - (none)
  • Dual - -riimb
  • Plural - -riimv

Alignment: Active-Stative

Word Order: APV

Cases: Agentive, Patientive, Dative, Instrumental, Ornative, Comitative, Oblique

Agentive case - used for verb arguments that are actively doing something

Patientive case - used for verb arguments that are describing state or change

Questions

Put the verb at the beginning of the sentence for a yes/no question

For other questions, use the word ziimd in place of whatever information you would like to know (put appropriate endings on it too, and this word can also be used for verbs)

Adjectives/Adverbs/Prepositions

  1. Add ending to the word (-çis for fact, -mêêmç for opinion)
  2. Add ending of noun or verb you wish to modify to the adjective/adverb/prep
  3. Place it before the noun or verb
  4. For adverbs/prepositions describing verb, time-manner-place order
  5. The objects of prepositions take no case ending

Prefixes

All

  • Opposite - hjey-

Verb -> Verb

  • Opposite (agentive and patientive switch) - my-
  • Movement towards - fy-
  • Movement away - vy-

Verb -> Noun

  • The act of doing (verb) - miim-
  • Place where (verb) is typically done - beemb-
  • Time where (verb) is typically done - deb-
  • The gender of all nouns made this way is always bmihji

Verb -> Adjective

  • Doing (verb) often - çe
  • Should be doing (verb) - rês

Noun -> Noun

  • Place typically with lots of (noun) - beemb-
  • Time typically with lots of (noun) - deb-
  • Polite - sys-
  • The gender of all nouns made this way is always the same as the root

Noun -> Verb

  • To act like (noun) - hçiimj-
  • To be similar to (noun) - mêi-

Noun -> Adjective

  • Having lots of (noun) - fyymç-
  • Being associated with (noun) - miid-

Adjective -> Noun

  • The state or quality of (adjective) - dyd-
  • The gender of all nouns made this way is always fri

Adjective -> Verb

  • To be (adjective) - diimz-

Diminutive and Augmentative

  • Diminutive - reduplicate last syllable of root and add to root
  • Augmentative - same as diminutive except change first vowel to y if e, ê, or i, and change to i if y
