Alphabet:

d - /ð/
f - /f/, /p̪/ if it comes after "m" in a syllable
v - /v/, /b̪/ if it comes after "m" in a syllable
s - /s/
z - /z/
ç - /ç/, /c/ if it comes after "m" in a syllable
j - /ʝ/, /ɟ/ if it comes after "m" in a syllable
m - /m/, /ɱ/ if it comes before "f" or "v" in a syllable
r - /ɾ/
i - /i/
y - /y/
e - /e/
ê - /æ/, replaces an "e" that comes after "m" or "r"

Digraphs:
hç - /cç/
hj - /ɟʝ/

Phonotactics: C1(r/m)V1(V2)(m)(C2) where
C1: any consonant
r/m: only if C1 isn't r, m, hç, or hj
V1: any vowel (except cannot be e after m or r, use ê instead)
V2: must be same vowel as V1 if second m exists
m: only if V1 and V2 both exist and are the same vowel
C2: any consonant except r, m, hç or hj
Stress always on second syllable of root

Genders: zmêfmy, brêd, zys, bmihji, fri

Agentive case - used for verb arguments that are actively doing something
Patientive case - used for verb arguments that are describing state or change

Questions:
Put the verb at the beginning of the sentence for a yes/no question
For other questions, use the word ziimd in place of whatever information you
would like to know (put appropriate endings on it too, and this word can also
be used for verbs)

Adjectives/Adverbs/Prepositions:
1. Add ending to the word (-çis for fact, -mêêmç for opinion)
2. Add ending of noun or verb you wish to modify to the adjective/adverb/prep
3. Place it before the noun or verb
4. For adverbs/prepositions describing verb, time-manner-place order
5. The objects of prepositions take no case ending

Augmentative - same as diminutive except change first vowel to y if e, ê, or i, and change to i if y
